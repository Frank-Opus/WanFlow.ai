## Design Context

### Users
- Primary external users are enterprise decision-makers, delivery leads, and business owners evaluating WanFlow as an AI implementation partner.
- Primary internal and product-side users are operators, benchmark engineers, delivery managers, and platform teams using the system to run evaluation, governance, and execution workflows.
- The job to be done is not to impress with a demo, but to help people quickly understand that WanFlow can turn data, workflow, agents, and model operations into something stable enough to deploy, operate, and hand over.

### Brand Personality
- Steady, restrained, trustworthy.
- Chinese-first but globally legible, formal enough for enterprise buyers without sounding cold or bureaucratic.
- The tone should feel like a serious AI delivery company: technically strong, operationally grounded, and credible in front of traditional business leaders who may not speak in AI-native language.
- The interface should evoke confidence, control, clarity, and delivery certainty.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Bright, editorial-enterprise presentation on a light canvas. The current implemented direction of Chinese serif display typography, clean sans-serif body text, blue and cyan interaction accents, white or translucent panels, and premium corporate rhythm is the canonical reference.
- Marketing pages should feel polished and brand-forward, while internal platform surfaces should feel precise and efficient; both should still share the same typography, spacing discipline, palette family, and component grammar.
- Use cool neutrals, disciplined blue accents, and only limited warm highlights for signal, trust, or emphasis. Favor clarity over spectacle.
- Explicit anti-references: generic dark AI landing pages, neon purple glow aesthetics, interchangeable SaaS dashboards, consultant-slide visuals, bloated gradients, and meta explanatory copy that reads like internal reasoning.
- Motion is allowed, but it must stay controlled, purposeful, and non-disruptive. Mobile layouts must protect readability first, and interfaces should respect reduced-motion preferences when animation is present.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with business readability. Enterprise visitors should understand what WanFlow does, where it fits, and why it is trustworthy within a few seconds, even if they are not AI experts.
2. Make AI feel operational, not theatrical. Show agents, automation, governance, and optimization as controllable business capabilities rather than abstract intelligence claims.
3. Keep hierarchy disciplined. Short directional headings, human body copy, strong section contrast, and no oversized blocks of vague marketing text.
4. Preserve one visual language across the repo. Marketing pages may be more expressive and platform pages more functional, but typography, containment, spacing, and color logic must still feel like one company.
5. Design for enterprise confidence on every screen. Responsive behavior, text overflow handling, reduced-motion support, and state clarity matter as much as visual polish.
